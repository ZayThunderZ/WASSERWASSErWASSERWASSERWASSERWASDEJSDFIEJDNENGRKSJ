How to drink water

The oldest instruction in the world, in five unhurried steps.

  1. Find a clean vessel

    A glass, a bottle, cupped hands in a pinch. Water isn't picky about what holds it.

  2. Fill it with fresh, cool water

    Tap water is fine in most places — let it run for a second first so it's cold and clear.

  3. Bring it to your lips

    Tilt the vessel slowly until the water meets your mouth. There's no need to hurry the ocean.

  4. Sip, don't gulp

    Smaller mouthfuls are easier to absorb and gentler on you than racing to the bottom of the glass.

  5. Repeat through the day

    Drink roughly when you're thirsty, and a little more in heat or after exercise. Your body keeps good track.

Rough target: around 2 litres a day suits many adults, but your body, climate, and activity set the real number. Pale-yellow is the colour you're aiming for.
